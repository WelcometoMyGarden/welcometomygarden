## Contribution guidelines

If you have a feature request, contact our team at support@welcometomygarden.org. If you're a WTMG member, you can also [post a topic](https://community.welcometomygarden.org/c/feedback/12) in our community space to include the community in the conversation.

Bug reports are welcome in [Issues](https://github.com/WelcometoMyGarden/welcometomygarden/issues).

### Getting started with developing for WTMG

To set up the development environment, check the [development environment docs](./docs/dev-env.md).

**Please review the below FAQ questions first** before working on a PR.

### Conventions

For commit messages, we use [Conventional Commits](https://www.conventionalcommits.org/en/v1.0.0/#specification) again since Jan 25th, 2023, with [extension types](https://github.com/angular/angular/blob/22b96b9/CONTRIBUTING.md#type) defined by Angular.

In addition, we use the `copy` type for commits that solely contain manual translation edits & additions.

## FAQ

### Can I help with fixing bug X?

_Yes, thank you!_ We appreciate it very much if you want to help with making WTMG better.

Thor will try to keep the GitHub Issues up-to-date with known bugs, issues, and defined feature ideas where we explicitly invite external contributions. These can be smaller UX paper cuts, as well as things we would really like to implement, but could not prioritize yet.

### I want to add feature X or change feature Y, will you accept my PR?

For smaller changes that fix an issue that we weren't aware of, but that otherwise don't change the WTMG experience significantly, we will likely accept your PR. Reviews could take some time though.

If you want to build a completely new feature, or change an existing feature significantly, please contact us first (see above) with your idea and the motivation behind it. We'd love to discuss it, but we likely won't accept an implementation before we're convinced that WTMG needs your idea in the way that you proposed it.

We generally follow a internal development workflow starting with the analysis of community suggestions and data to support a need or requirement. Next, we iteratively design a solution, reviewing intermediate stages with representatives of our community. The final stages consist of coordinated implementation, testing and communication by our team.

An idea may drop off at any stage in the above process.

Larger changes can impact the (social) dynamics or experience of the platform, and we want to make sure that WTMG keeps serving its mission in the best way possible. What seems like a purely useful addition at first sight might turn out to be an annoying change for some community members, or make the platform less simple to use after review.

We also have very finite resources, which we assign carefully in monthly cycles following [Shape Up methodology](https://basecamp.com/shapeup). For larger changes, we need to ensure the proper cohesion and coordination of the platform in terms of design, development and communication. Even if you can help with implementation, we are likely not immediately able to deploy our time to handle all the related work.

Rest assured though that we take note of any suggestions you make! Your idea might already be on our wishlist for the future in some form, and we will likely get back to you at some point to discuss your idea more deeply, or to ask you to review or test our design or implementation. Maybe then implementation contributions are welcome, you could be the first to make a big technical mark on WTMG!

### Can I use AI tools to contribute?

Yes, but please disclose in your PR that you did so. Also mention which tools you used.

Whenever you submit code (partially) written by AI, review and test the changes completely and manually yourself to ensure their quality.

Completely autonomously generated "fixes" that you did not review may contain obvious or subtle errors, of which the latter are worse. They are not appreciated as they waste our time. Luckily we haven't seen such cases here yet, let's keep it that way!
